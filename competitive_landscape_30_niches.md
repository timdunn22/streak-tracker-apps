*Compiled February 5, 2026*

## 1. QUIT SMOKING APPS

**Top Competitors:**
| App | Rating | Reviews | Subscription Price |
|-----|--------|---------|-------------------|
| Smoke Free | 4.8 | ~150K+ | Pro $4.99 one-time; subscription tiers available |
| Kwit | 4.7 | ~45K+ | ~$5/mo or ~10 EUR premium |
| QuitNow! | 4.6 | ~30K+ | Free w/ premium tiers |

**Revenue Estimates:** Smoke Free generated ~$219K/month at peak (Sensor Tower Q2 2024), with ~2.3M downloads in a single month. Over 4M total users across 190 countries.

**Common 1-2 Star Complaints:**
- Inaccurate health recovery timelines (e.g., lung cancer risk claims)
- Aggressive ads and paywalling basic features
- Generic, non-personalized advice (only 18% of apps offer personalized content per research)
- App crashes, especially when dismissing ads
- Timer/tracking glitches

**Crowdedness: MODERATE**

**Gap/Opportunity:** Only 18% of quit smoking apps offer personalized quit plans. Most rely on generic timelines. There is an opening for an app that uses AI to personalize the quit journey, tracks real withdrawal symptoms with adaptive support, and avoids the ad-heavy freemium model that plagues this space. The government-backed quitSTART is free but outdated. A modern, clinically-backed app with a clean UX could differentiate.

## 2. SOBRIETY / QUIT DRINKING APPS

**Top Competitors:**
| App | Rating | Reviews | Subscription Price |
|-----|--------|---------|-------------------|
| I Am Sober | 4.8 | ~200K+ (5-star) | Free; Sober Plus subscription available |
| Reframe | 4.8 | ~50K+ | $14/mo or $140/yr |

**Revenue Estimates:** I Am Sober: ~300K downloads/mo, ~$200K revenue/mo (Sensor Tower Dec 2025). Unfunded, bootstrapped by 2 founders in Seattle. Reframe crossed $10M ARR with ~100K paying subscribers (2022 data).

**Common 1-2 Star Complaints:**
- Community features can be toxic or triggering
- Premium paywalls for core motivational features
- Notifications can feel pushy or guilt-inducing
- Limited support for non-alcohol addictions in alcohol-focused apps
- Data sync issues across devices

**Crowdedness: MODERATE**

**Gap/Opportunity:** I Am Sober dominates but is bootstrapped and slow to innovate. Reframe is expensive at $14/mo. There's a gap for a mid-price ($3-5/mo) app that combines I Am Sober's simplicity with better community moderation, CBT-based tools, and health recovery tracking. Apps for "moderation" (cutting back vs. full sobriety) are underexplored.

One Sec's behavioral "friction" approach (deep breath before opening apps) is brilliant and underexplored. An app combining behavioral nudges (One Sec-style) with clean analytics (Opal-style) at a lower price point ($30-40/yr) could compete.
